# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

EVE is a platform for building **safe, deterministic and trustworthy AI systems**.
We are committed to providing a respectful, professional and inclusive environment
for everyone who engages with this project.

This applies to all interactions within the EVE ecosystem, including:
- GitHub issues and discussions
- Pull requests and code reviews
- Documentation feedback
- Community conversations related to EVE

---

## Our Standards

We expect all participants to:

- Be respectful and constructive in communication
- Assume good intent from others
- Focus on ideas, architecture and engineering quality — not individuals
- Accept that EVE prioritizes **safety, determinism and human control** over speed or hype
- Respect that parts of the EVE platform are intentionally private

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- Harassment, insults, or personal attacks
- Pressuring maintainers to open-source private core components
- Misrepresenting EVE’s goals, scope or guarantees
- Using the project to promote unsafe or deceptive AI practices

---

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying standards and enforcing this code of conduct.
They may remove content, comments or participants that violate these principles.

Serious or repeated violations may result in a ban from the repository.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within this repository and in public spaces
when an individual is representing or discussing the EVE platform.

---

## Contact

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it by
opening a private issue or contacting the maintainer directly.

---

EVE exists to raise the standard for AI systems.
That starts with how we treat each other.
